Interview problem: LRU Cache. I kept getting O(n) on the update operation because I was using a list to track recency. The correct solution is to combine a hashmap with a doubly linked list. The hashmap gives O(1) lookup by key. The doubly linked list gives O(1) insertion and deletion for recency ordering. When a key is accessed, you remove it from its current position in the list and move it to the front — all in O(1) because you have a direct pointer from the hashmap to the node. The key insight is to separate two responsibilities: lookup and ordering. Trying to handle both with one data structure is what causes the O(n) trap.
