We had a privilege escalation risk in the OAuth Client Credentials Grant. A service was able to request tokens with scopes beyond what it was registered for, because the scope validation was happening after token issuance instead of before. The fix was to move the scope check earlier in the flow — validate first, then issue the token. We also added stricter boundary checks at the service level. Lesson: in authorization flows, always validate permissions before granting anything. The order of operations matters a lot in security-sensitive code. After the fix we added SLO alerts to catch any future anomalies early.
